<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1"><strong><em>Top ten rules for using syrups in a caf&eacute;;</em></strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1"><strong><em><br /> </em></strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">1. Adding a flavour for an extra charge doesn't work - create a drink!</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">2. Use strong visuals of the drink - a picture can beat a thousand words don&rsquo;t forget :)</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">3. Put a name on the drink but be careful don't make it too poncy! Would you ask for an alpine topped mocha with swirl?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">4. Unless you are in a very developed market for syrups don't display a long line on the back bar.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">5. Fruit flavours generally don't work with hot drinks but can with cold. Unless designed for them; such as orange syrup &amp; hot chocolate or forest fruits &amp; hot chocolate (black forest hot chocolate)</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">6. Flavour your cream - vanilla is great in your general whipped cream and chocolate flavoured cream can add a premium to your hot chocolate. Here i am making the assumption that you are using a professional cream whipper not one of those horrid uht squirties.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">7. Promote - have a free flavour friday occasionally. The investment will repay in repeat sales.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">8. Do it properly - there is nothing worse than a drink either too heavily flavoured or not flavoured enough.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">9. Charge a sensible mark up for your flavoured creations. At a cost of under 5p (if you are paying more you should be ordering from us &ndash; hey if you have got this far you should be ordering anyway! - See your syrups as a margin maximiser not as a deterrent.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;" data-mce-mark="1">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva;">10. Don't forget children - flavours work great in drinks for them. Raspberry syrup in a glass of sparkling water gives a great profit booster that&rsquo;s far healthier than many soft drinks too!</span></p>
